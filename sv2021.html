SN 2021fxy: An Unreddened Cousin of SN 2017erp?

About This Work

The goal of this project is to analyze SN 2021fxy, a Type Ia supernova followed by POISE, for which we also obtained UV spectra using the Hubble Space Telescope. These HST spectra reveal unexpected similarities between SN 2021fxy and SN 2017erp, as the two objects are categorized as "shallow-silicon" and "core-normal" objects respectively within the Branch scheme. As differences between otherise similar SNe Ia are expected to become more prominent in the UV, detailed study of these two objects allow us to probe variations of the density and metallicity ion the outermost ejecta, where the UV specrum forms. When combined with the optical and NIR dataset from POISE, we can better understand the connections between different Branch sub-groups and gain a deeper understanding of the nature of the progenitor systems and explosion mechanisms in SNe Ia.

NOTE: All plots and conclusions presented here are preliminary and subject to change.

Discovery and Follow-up

SN 2021fxy was discovered by Koichi Itagaki at 2021-03-17 17:23:17 UT in NGC 5018. ASAS-SN has a last non detection at 2021-03-15.4511418 UT with a limiting magnitude of 17.95 mag in g-band (retrieved from ASAS-SN Sky Portal). It was quickly confirmed as a young SN Ia by SIRAH, with a Si II velocity of 18,000 km/s (Jha 2021). POISE obtained its first two photometric points the following night, and continued follow-up using the Swope 1m telescope at Las Campanas and the Las Cumbres Global Telescope Network. Spectroscopic follow-up NOT 2.5m telescope, the ARC 3.5m telescope at Apache Point, and the 6m Magellan Clay telescope at Las Campanas.

SN 2021fxy is the third SNe Ia discovered in NGC 5018. The first is SN 2002dj, a "broad-line" SN Ia that was well characterized by Pignata et al. (2008). The other SN 2017isq, which was discovered approximately one month after maximum light, and may be associated with the stellar stream between NGC 5018 and NGC 5022.
