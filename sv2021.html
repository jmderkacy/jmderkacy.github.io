SN 2021fxy: An Unreddened Cousin of SN 2017erp?

About This Work

The goal of this project is to analyze SN 2021fxy, a Type Ia supernova followed by POISE, for which we also obtained UV spectra using the Hubble Space Telescope. These HST spectra reveal unexpected similarities between SN 2021fxy and SN 2017erp, as the two objects are categorized as "shallow-silicon" and "core-normal" objects respectively within the Branch scheme. As differences between otherise similar SNe Ia are expected to become more prominent in the UV, detailed study of these two objects allow us to probe variations of the density and metallicity in the outermost ejecta, where the UV spectrum forms. When combined with the optical and NIR dataset from POISE, we can better explore the connections between different Branch sub-groups and gain a deeper understanding of the nature of the progenitor systems and explosion mechanisms in SNe Ia.

NOTE: All plots and conclusions presented here are preliminary and subject to change.

Discovery and Follow-up

SN 2021fxy was discovered by Koichi Itagaki at 2021-03-17 17:23:17 UT in NGC 5018. ASAS-SN has a last non detection at 2021-03-15.4511418 UT with a limiting magnitude of 17.95 mag in g-band (retrieved from ASAS-SN Sky Portal). It was quickly confirmed as a young SN Ia by SIRAH, with a Si II velocity of 18,000 km/s (Jha 2021). POISE obtained its first two photometric points the following night, and continued follow-up using the Swope 1m telescope at Las Campanas and the Las Cumbres Global Telescope Network. Spectroscopic follow-up was performed with the NOT 2.5m telescope, the ARC 3.5m telescope at Apache Point, and the 6m Magellan Clay telescope at Las Campanas.

SN 2021fxy is the third SNe Ia discovered in NGC 5018. The first is SN 2002dj, a "broad-line" SN Ia that was well characterized by Pignata et al. (2008). The other SN 2017isq, which was discovered approximately one month after maximum light, and may be associated with the stellar stream between NGC 5018 and nearby NGC 5022.

SYNOW Velocities


Using Spextractor and the SNIaDCA code package from Burrow et al (2020), we place both SN 2017erp and SN 2021fxy on the Branch diagram using their maximum light spectra. SN 2021fxy is classified as a shallow silicon with psuedo-equivalents widths for the 6355 A and 5792 A features of 80 A and 18 A respectively, using the 2D Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) . In the figure below, its denoted by the yellow-green star, where the coloring is determined by the probability of its inclusion in the various groups (see Burrow et al., 2020 for more details). The red star indicates the location of SN 2017erp. It should be noted that SN 2021fxy falls in the region where the 2-sigma boundaries of both the shallow-silicon and core-normal groups overlap. Several of the nearby objects in the Burrow sample have varying classifications depending on the number of variables included in the GMM, suggesting that the two groups share some underlying properties.

Max Light UV/Optical Spectra Comparison


Taking a closer look at the optical, we see that while SN 2017erp is redder than SN 2021fxy, the blue half of both spectra are incredibly similar, and despite the flux differences in the red half of the spectra, most of the lines have similar profiles and relative depths. The one noteable exception is the Si II lines, where the line profiles and depths are distinctly different at early times and slowly evolve to become more consistent post-maximum light. This suggests that while the interior portions of the ejects have many similarities, there must be significant enough differences to result in the different Branch groupings of SN 2017erp and SN 2021fxy. This level of difference is not observed in our sequence of UV spectra, suggesting that the outer layers are much more similar than the interiors.
